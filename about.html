LEGAL INSTRUMENT

CAMIFUCOS, The CAMEROON INDUSTRIAL FARMERS’ UNION OF COOPERATIVE SOCIETIES is a national dynamic civil society and unique community. It is also an institutional treaty framework that defines and secures the blessings of Members liberty, economic investment, and services, and defines and secures:

  • industry
  • commerce
  • financial independence

It operates within the policy framework set by the Cameroon Government under the Minister of Agriculture and Rural Development and the Minister of Livestock, Fisheries and Animal Industries.

AIMS and OBJECTIVES

CAMIFUCOS sets a path that leads thousands of its members into the 21st century by:

  • more sustainable, competitive, and equitable economic development
  • orientation of technological development
  • institutional change and framework development
  • income-generating capacity-building projects
  • food self-sufficiency and economic growth
  • protection of our environment and thus managing agricultural holdings that use “Organic Manure”
  • environmentally sound crop farming practices with major restriction on the use of chemical fertilizers

SET-UP and MANAGEMENT

The Business Organisational structure of CAMIFUCOS is managed and governed voluntarily, impartially in good faith and directly by two Administrative and Management Segments of legal thoughtful Board Members and Members of the Supervisory Council of all walks of life who jointly implement decisions adopted in the CAMIFUCOS’ Annual Ordinary General Assembly of Delegates (the supreme body) from CAMIFUCOS’ many further dynamic growth cooperative societies and little knots of group farm-families. The decisions of the supreme body are thus implemented by the administrative and management apex organ including a Director-General, in good conduct platform avoiding the granting of legal aid in this case in conformity with the principles and practices of CAMIFU-COS’ Articles of Association and world-wide-network standards based on justice, trust, reliability, dependability, confidentiality and democratically over all strategies between the Producers of Agro-Pastoral capital goods and Consumers, and the chief element being competitiveness/competition proportionately to the strength of each Member’s supply , demand and profit.
